## Why do it?

Nobody knows everything. Cybersecurity evolves quickly: new CVEs, new tools, new evasion techniques. The community is where this knowledge is shared in near real-time, often before it is officially published.

## What happens next?

You will have access to a network of professionals and enthusiasts to solve complex problems, receive feedback on configurations, discover new tools and stay updated on emerging vulnerabilities and techniques.

## What's the risk if you don't?

Professional isolation and much longer times to solve problems that others have already faced and documented. In cybersecurity, staying updated is an operational necessity, not an option.

---

> **Note:** Active community participation (answering questions, reporting bugs, contributing to open source tools) is one of the most effective ways to build professional visibility in the field. Platforms like GitHub, HackTheBox and TryHackMe with public profiles and rankings are concrete elements to include in a professional portfolio.
